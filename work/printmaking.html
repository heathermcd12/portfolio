Series

Fall of 2015, I took New Printmaking at the Ottawa School of Art, taught by Guillermo Trejo. In it, I have learned how relaxing it is to be away from a computer and to work with your hands to create something unique. With my illustration style, I have had a lot of fun experimenting with etchings, dry point and linocuts.

Blue Cat - Series of 4

This small series of linocut prints were printed twice after the first went through the press, creating ghost prints. The ink I printed with had a mild transparency which lightened the opacity and made it easier to layer.

Sparrow, Montreal. Series of 6

My first time mixing relief and intaglio printing. This design is of a pedestrian view of Sparrow, a cozy restaurant on St.Laurent Blvd in Montreal. The print shown is my favourite due to its imperfections.

Record girl, Series of 3

First time experimenting with etching. The process for etching was much more time consuming than lino, but offered more room for details. I loved doing this series because of the size of the plate and the amount of lines.
